Rhythm games, Dance Dance Revolution especially, were my world for years. I was internationally recognized both for playing and for thinking hard about what made gameplay good, spending that time authoring custom step charts for songs in free DDR simulators and eventually writing a thesis on step chart design: what makes a pattern easy or hard to perform, and how to give a song character.

That is what brought Roxor to me. I worked first on In The Groove 2 for PC, then on In The Groove 3, slated for an arcade release, authoring roughly 70% of its charts before that release was derailed by Roxor's legal battle with Konami. The charts still live on across other mediums.
