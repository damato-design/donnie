Some approaches here are highly experimental and remain only as proofs of concept, so user discretion is advised. The throughline is that trust is built from the smallest pieces up: when a token or component behaves as expected, the larger experience earns confidence, and a novel concept that breaks that expectation spends it.

Two of those experiments shape the everyday details. Density follows a [complementary space](https://blog.damato.design/posts/complementary-space/) model, where a region's scope, not individual tokens, describes how compact it is, so nested composition grows tighter on its own without separate size variants. Headings use a [fluid](https://blog.damato.design/posts/fluid-headings/) approach that shows the logic-over-feeling rule in action. Designers curating headings by feel were already, accidentally, keeping each heading's overall composition, the way its lines wrap and break, even as screens got smaller. The fluid rule makes that deliberate instead of incidental; `clamp()` is just how it gets there.

Explore it at [system.damato.design](https://system.damato.design).
