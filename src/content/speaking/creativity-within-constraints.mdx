In the design community, constraints are often seen as creativity's enemy, a cage that stifles imagination. But what if that's exactly backward? Drawing on Karl Duncker's Candle Problem and the global sensation of MacGyver, this talk explores functional fixedness and argues that embracing constraints, user needs, product requirements, brand guidelines, technical boundaries, unlocks a richer, more focused kind of creativity. Design systems aren't meant to fight creativity; they're the scaffolding for consistent, user-centered solutions.
