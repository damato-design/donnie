---
title: "Avoid z-index Escalation in the System"
date: 2023-12-11
context: "Stacking conflicts tempt teams into an arms race of ever-larger z-index values, 'my thing is more important than your thing', until the numbers are meaningless and overlays fight each other unpredictably."
decision: "Treat reaching for a higher z-index as a smell. Prefer source order, scoped stacking contexts, and a small named set of layers over ad-hoc numeric escalation."
alternatives:
  - option: "Bump z-index whenever something needs to sit on top"
    pros:
      - "Immediate fix for the symptom"
    cons:
      - "Numbers escalate without bound"
      - "No shared meaning, every value is a guess"
      - "Overlays compete unpredictably"
  - option: "A single global z-index scale everyone references"
    pros:
      - "Some shared order"
    cons:
      - "Still encourages thinking in magnitudes"
      - "Global coupling between unrelated components"
  - option: "Source order + scoped stacking contexts + a few named layers"
    pros:
      - "Removes the numbers war"
      - "Stacking becomes local and predictable"
      - "Few, intentional layers (e.g. base, overlay, modal)"
    cons:
      - "Requires understanding stacking contexts"
      - "Occasionally needs markup reordering"
reasoning: "z-index escalation is a symptom of missing structure. Establishing stacking contexts and a small, named set of layers makes overlay behavior predictable and keeps the system from devolving into a meaningless numbers war."
whyItMattered: >-
  When everything is `z-index: 9999`, nothing is. Designing the stacking model
  up front, rather than reacting with bigger numbers, keeps overlays sane as a
  system grows.
