---
title: "Shift the Component Library Toward Composable Primitives"
date: 2021-06-14
context: "UXCore had grown through a contributor model, accumulating many components with questionable or duplicated use across the organization. The catalog was large, inconsistent, and expensive to maintain."
decision: "Steer the library away from an ever-growing catalog of bespoke components toward a smaller set of highly composable primitives that teams combine into richer experiences."
alternatives:
  - option: "Keep accepting contributed components"
    pros:
      - "Low friction for contributing teams"
      - "Covers many specific use cases out of the box"
    cons:
      - "Duplication and one-off components"
      - "Inconsistent quality and maintenance burden"
  - option: "Freeze the library and let teams fork"
    pros:
      - "Stops the growth immediately"
    cons:
      - "Fragmentation across the org"
      - "Loses the value of a shared system"
  - option: "Composable primitives teams combine themselves"
    pros:
      - "Fewer, higher-quality building blocks"
      - "Scales to more experiences than a fixed catalog"
      - "Lower long-term maintenance"
    cons:
      - "Requires deprecating popular components"
      - "Shifts some assembly work to consumers"
reasoning: "A small set of composable pieces covers more ground than a sprawling catalog of single-purpose components, and it dramatically lowers maintenance. Deprecation is real design system work; curating down is as valuable as adding."
whyItMattered: >-
  Composability turned the library from a thing you pick from into a thing you
  build with. It's slower-burning work, much of it ongoing, but it's how a
  component library stays maintainable as the organization scales.
